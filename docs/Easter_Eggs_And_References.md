### Irene Newman (1875-1958)
**"The World's First Dental Hygienist"**

- **Significance:** Trained by Dr. Alfred Fones in 1906, became the first licensed dental hygienist in the world
- **Legacy:** Established dental hygiene as a profession focused on prevention (oral prophylaxis and education) rather than just treatment
- **Why She Matters:** Pioneer who fought to establish dental hygiene as respected "women's work" when dentistry was male-dominated
- **Connection to Our Project:** EDHS practitioners in Oregon continue her legacy of independent preventive care

**Where She Appears:**
- Primary test practitioner in `PractitionerTests.cs`
- Location: Bridgeport, CT (where she actually practiced)
- Phone: 503-555-1906 (year she became first dental hygienist)
- Services: "Oral Prophylaxis, Oral Hygiene Education, Preventive Care"
- Practice Name: "Fones School of Dental Hygiene"

**Learn More:** [Wikipedia - Irene Newman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irene_Newman)

### Dr. Alfred Civilion Fones (1869-1938)
**"The Father of Dental Hygiene"**

- **Significance:** Dentist who trained Irene Newman and founded the first dental hygiene school in 1913
- **Legacy:** Championed prevention over treatment, believed oral health education should be accessible to all
- **Why He Matters:** Male ally who elevated dental hygiene from "assistant work" to a recognized profession
- **Philosophy:** "It is easier and better to prevent disease than to cure it"

**Where He Appears:**
- Test practitioner representing a practice owner/mentor figure
- Referenced in "Fones School of Dental Hygiene" practice name
- (Future: Will use in seed data for mentor/practice owner scenarios)

**Learn More:** [Fones School of Dental Hygiene](https://www.qu.edu/schools/school-of-health-sciences/academics/dental-hygiene/)

## üó∫Ô∏è Oregon Connection

While our pioneers practiced on the East Coast, Oregon has been a leader in expanding dental hygiene scope of practice:

- **1995:** Oregon passes legislation allowing independent practice for dental hygienists (one of the first states)
- **EDHS License:** Expanded Practice Dental Hygienist credential allows practice without dentist supervision
- **400+ EDHS practitioners** in Oregon today (compared to ~50 in most states)

**EDHS Finder continues the legacy** of making preventive dental care accessible, independent, and patient-centered.
